<h2>What Makes Classic Sunglasses Timeless?</h2>
<p><b>Classic sunglasses</b> have <em>timeless designs</em> that have made their mark in fashion history. They are loved for their beauty and deep cultural meaning. These iconic frames have lasted through the years, becoming a key part of <em>classic eyewear trends</em>.</p>
<p><b>Aviator</b> sunglasses, created in the 1930s by Bausch and Lomb, became very popular in the 1970s. Celebrities like John Lennon and David Bowie made golden and amber lenses cool. Jackie Kennedy also wore oversized black sunglasses, showing how versatile they are.</p>
<p>In the 1970s, sunglasses moved from glass to plastic lenses. This brought thick frames that were both tough and comfy. The 1970s also saw double-bridge styles become a must-have for both men and women. Bold tortoiseshell patterns became popular, moving from natural to synthetic for a greener choice.</p>
<p>The cat-eye shape is another classic that never goes out of style. Stars like Marilyn Monroe and Audrey Hepburn wore them, making them a favorite. Classic sunglasses keep changing but their <em>cultural significance</em> and flexibility in fashion keep them loved by all ages.</p>
