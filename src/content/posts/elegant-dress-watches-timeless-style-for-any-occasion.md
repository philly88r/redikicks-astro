<h2>History and Evolution of Dress Watches</h2>
<p>The story of dress watches is fascinating. It started in the early 1900s when wristwatches took over from pocket watches. They were first for the military, then loved by everyone for being useful and stylish. Over time, <b>luxury watches</b> became more elegant, marking important moments.</p>
<p>By the 1800s, British watchmakers made half of the world’s watches, about 200,000 each year. But by 1900, this number dropped to 100,000 due to economic changes and world events. Britain was a big name in watch innovation, with 60-70% of new ideas coming from there.</p>
<p>During World War I, big changes happened in men&#8217;s watches. They added features like glow-in-the-dark hands and strong glass. These changes made watches more reliable and helped shape the look of classic watches.</p>
<p>In 2021, Bremont introduced the Longitude Limited Edition watch. It used old brass from the Royal Observatory Greenwich. This showed a mix of history and new design. Bremont also opened a new factory in Henley-on-Thames to make watch parts.</p>
<p>Old dress watches were usually 30-36 mm in size, sometimes up to 38-40 mm. They were simple and elegant. Now, modern dress watches are more practical and stylish. They blend formal and casual styles together.</p>
<p>Today, you can find dress watches for under $1,000. Brands like Hugo Boss and Armani offer stylish options at good prices. <b>Luxury watches</b> have changed a lot, offering more choices for everyone.</p>
