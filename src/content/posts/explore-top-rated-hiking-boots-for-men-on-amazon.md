<h2>Care and Maintenance Tips</h2>
<p>Proper <strong>Hiking Boot Care</strong> is key to keeping your outdoor shoes in great shape. Regular tasks like cleaning, conditioning, and waterproofing can make your hiking boots last longer. They will also be ready for your next adventure.</p>
<p>Dave Page, who runs a boot repair shop in Seattle for 40 years, suggests breaking in new leather boots with short hikes first. This helps the leather get used to your feet. It ensures you get the best <em>comfort and fit</em>.</p>
<p>To keep your leather boots soft, use Nikwax&#8217;s Conditioner for Leather. For <em>waterproofing</em>, Page recommends reapplying a DWR treatment to leather and nylon boots. The original coating can fade over time. Nikwax&#8217;s Fabric and Leather Proof is a good choice that doesn&#8217;t use PFC.</p>
<p>Good <strong>Hiking Boot Maintenance</strong> means taking out the insoles after each hike. This stops moisture from building up and prevents bacteria from growing. Also, clean and air-dry your boots after each use. This keeps them strong and stops them from breaking down when stored.</p>
