<h2>Introduction to Quilted Jackets</h2>
<p><b>Quilted jackets</b> have a long history filled with tradition and practical use. The art of quilting, which stitches together multiple fabric layers, started in 12th century England. This method not only looks great but also keeps you warm, making <b>quilted jackets</b> a classic choice for cold weather.</p>
<h3>History of Quilted Jackets</h3>
<p>The quilted jacket&#8217;s story begins with the <em>quilting tradition</em> from the Middle Ages. Quilting was first for making padded armor and protective clothes. It used the fabric layers&#8217; insulating properties. Later, quilted jackets became popular for their looks and warmth.</p>
<h3>The Original Husky Jacket</h3>
<p>The <em>Husky Jacket</em> is a famous quilted jacket design, created by RefrigiWear over 70 years ago. They made it for people facing extreme cold, using special insulation for lasting warmth.</p>
<p>Now, quilted jackets are a key part of <b>men&#8217;s fashion</b>, mixing timeless style with practical use. They range from classic British styles to modern Italian designs. This makes them a favorite for men wanting both <b>warmth and style</b>.</p>

<h3>What is the history of quilted jackets?</h3>
<div>
<div>
<p>Quilting is a stitching method that combines two or more layers of cloth. It often includes a padding layer in the middle. This creates the quilted look we know today. The word &#8216;quilt&#8217; comes from the Latin &#8216;cucita&#8217;, meaning cushion or bolster.</p>

<h3>What is the original Husky jacket?</h3>
<div>
<div>
