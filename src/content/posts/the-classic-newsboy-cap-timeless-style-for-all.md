<h2>A Brief History of the Newsboy Cap</h2>
<p>The <em>history of the newsboy cap</em> goes back to the late 1800s. It was made for working-class boys who sold newspapers. Soon, it became a common piece of clothing. The name &#8220;newsboy&#8221; came from its link to city kids.</p>
<p>In 1571, a law in Northern England made all males over six wear woolen hats on certain days. This was mainly for the working class. Wealthier folks preferred silk or velvet hats. The newsboy cap, a rounder and puffier version, appeared in the 1800s with a button on top.</p>
<p>The newsboy cap became famous thanks to the <em>Peaky Blinders</em>, a gang in Birmingham in the 1890s. They used it as a symbol of power and confidence. By the 1920s, it was popular among the upper class too, with fancier versions.</p>
