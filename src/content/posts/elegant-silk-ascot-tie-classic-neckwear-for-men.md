<h2>The History of the Ascot Tie</h2>
<p>The <b>history of ascot ties</b> is quite interesting. It started in the 17th century with military uniforms in Croatia. These early ties were made from fine fabrics like silk.</p>
<p>In late 19th-century England, the ascot tie became popular. It was loved by gentlemen who went to the Ascot Racecourse. The elegant silk ascot made a big impact, changing men&#8217;s neckwear with its unique style.</p>

<p>Looking after your silk ascot means cleaning and storing it right. Doing this keeps it looking great and lasting longer.</p>

<p>For silk ascot ties, dry cleaning is best. A pro with the right solvents keeps the silk shiny and soft. If you want to wash it yourself, use cool water and a special cleaner for silk or wool. Don&#8217;t use regular laundry detergent, as it can damage the silk.</p>

<h3>Storage Best Practices for Silk Ascots</h3>
<p>Storing silk ascots right is key. Roll or fold them to avoid stretching. Use a cravat rack or fabric bag to keep them dust-free and away from light, which can fade colors.</p>
