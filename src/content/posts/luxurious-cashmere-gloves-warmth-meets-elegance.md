<h2>How to Care for Cashmere Gloves</h2>
<p>Looking after your cashmere handwear keeps them looking great and lasting longer. It&#8217;s key to know how to wash, dry, and store them right.</p>
<h3>Washing and Drying Tips</h3>
<p>Don&#8217;t just go for dry cleaning for cashmere. It can hurt the soft fibers. Try hand washing your gloves with gentle soap or baby shampoo instead.</p>
<ol>
<li>Turn the gloves inside out.</li>
<li>Fill a basin with lukewarm water, about 35°.</li>
<li>Add the soap and gently lather the gloves without wringing them out.</li>
<li>Rinse well with clean water.</li>
<li>Lay the gloves flat on a clean towel to dry, away from heat.</li>
</ol>
<p>If you get a stain, clean it right away with soda water and a clean cloth. For machine washing, put the gloves in a mesh bag. Use a gentle detergent with cool water, then air-dry them flat.</p>
<h3>Storage Recommendations</h3>
<p>Storing your cashmere gloves right is key to keeping them in top shape. Use a cotton bag that lets air in for storage. Add cedar wood balls to keep moths away naturally.</p>
<p>Regularly check and de-pill your cashmere to keep it looking good. Keep them away from heat and sunlight when you&#8217;re not using them. This helps them last longer.</p>
