<h2>History of Wayfarers</h2>

<p>The Wayfarers&#44; an iconic symbol of cool and rebellion&#44; were first introduced in 1956 by Bausch &#38; Lomb&#44; designed by the visionary Raymond Stegeman. They quickly evolved into a mid-century design classic characterized by their unique trapezoidal shape and robust frame.</p>
<p>Their resurgence in popularity during the 1980s&#44; showcased in films like &#42;The Blues Brothers&#42; and &#42;Risky Business&#42;&#44; cemented the Wayfarer legacy as culturally influential throughout generations. By marrying functionality with bold aesthetics&#44; these sunglasses transcended mere fashion statements&#44; becoming emblematic of a lifestyle defined by freedom and individuality.</p>
<p>Though challenged by emerging brands in the 1990s&#44; their relaunch in 2001 under Luxottica reaffirmed their status as a quintessential accessory for the modern innovative consumer&#44; sustaining their relevance in contemporary style narratives.</p>
