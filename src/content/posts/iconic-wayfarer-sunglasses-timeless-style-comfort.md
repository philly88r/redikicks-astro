<h2>The History of Wayfarer Sunglasses</h2>
<p>Wayfarer sunglasses have a story filled with creativity, culture, and lasting popularity. They were introduced in 1952 by Ray-Ban, thanks to Raymond Stegman. These sunglasses quickly changed <b>fashion eyewear</b> forever. Their unique look and bold style started a new trend, loved by style icons and everyone else.</p>
<h3>Introduction in 1952</h3>
<p>Wayfarer sunglasses first appeared in the early &#8217;50s, setting a new standard for style. Ray-Ban&#8217;s launch of this model changed everything, making them a hit with people everywhere. Their new plastic frame was different from the metal ones before, catching everyone&#8217;s eye and changing fashion worldwide.</p>
<h3>Resurgence in the 1980s</h3>
<p>The &#8217;80s brought a big comeback for Wayfarer sunglasses. Pop culture icons helped make them popular again. Tom Cruise wearing them in &#8220;Risky Business&#8221; boosted sales a lot. By 1982, over 1.5 million pairs were sold, thanks to a deal with Bausch &amp; Lomb. Stars like Michael Jackson made them even more popular during his &#8220;Bad&#8221; tour.</p>
<h3>Continued Popularity in the 21st Century</h3>
<p>Wayfarer sunglasses stayed popular into the 2000s, showing they could adapt and stay stylish. In the late 2000s, Ray-Ban saw a big increase in sales, thanks to eBay and a new release of their classic style. Today, they are still a key part of fashion, blending history with modern style.</p>

<h2>Care and Maintenance for Your Wayfarer Sunglasses</h2>
<p>Keep your Wayfarer sunglasses looking great with the right care. Use a stylish hard case to protect them when not worn. This prevents scratches and damage. Also, rest your sunglasses upside down to keep the lenses safe.</p>
<p>Cleaning your lenses needs care. Use a microfiber cloth, not cotton or paper towels, to avoid scratches. Glass lenses in Ray-Ban sunglasses are more scratch-resistant than other materials. Always clean them with an optical cleaner without ammonia, as regular cleaners can be harmful.</p>
<p>Clean your sunglasses often. Rinse them under warm water and dry with a microfiber cloth. Start with the frame and then the lenses. Cleaning them every week or two, especially in summer, stops dust and dirt from building up. Things like sunscreen, makeup, and pollen can also harm your lenses.</p>
<ul>
<li>Don&#8217;t wipe lenses with shirt hems; dust may scratch them.</li>
<li>Avoid vinegar, bleach, or ammonia as they damage lenses.</li>
<li>Consider a quick clean using moistened lens wipes for convenience.</li>
<li>Keep your glasses away from extreme heat, such as inside a parked car.</li>
<li>Wash your microfiber cloth weekly to maintain its effectiveness.</li>
</ul>
<p>Your Wayfarer sunglasses can last over ten years with the right care. Choose polarized or mirrored lenses for easier cleaning and more durability. Keep different pairs in special spots to avoid losing them and keep your collection tidy.</p>
